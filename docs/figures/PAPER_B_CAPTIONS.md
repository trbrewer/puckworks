# Paper B — submission-ready figure captions

These captions are written to stand alone from the main text and retain the distinction between empirical observations, post-fit reconstruction, model capacity, and exploratory simulation. File stems match `puckworks.figures`.

## Figure 1 — Matched target and model capacity (`fig1_result1_tds_ey`)

**Figure 1. Source-derived TDS extraction yield and static-heterogeneity model capacity.** (a) TDS-derived extraction-yield values at grinder dials 1.4, 1.7, and 2.0 are source-derived run-level endpoints obtained from measured first fractions plus integration of fitted extraction kinetics to the target beverage mass. Points show independently prepared extraction runs; bars are cell means ± SD (`n=3`, `n=6` at the centre dial 1.7, and `n=3`, respectively). The conditional response-surface curve is refitted to the full 15-setting design using achieved flow and temperature; shading is its fixed-design residual-bootstrap 95% curve band and the vertical interval is the conditional vertex interval. The three selected cell means increase monotonically, whereas the retained quadratic surface has an interior vertex near dial 1.74. Achieved flow and pressure differ by dial, so neither contrast isolates a dial-only causal effect. (b) A unit-mean lognormal streamtube ensemble is evaluated on its own, non-portable Cameron grind axis. The ensemble can generate an interior maximum for selected closure settings while the homogeneous response remains monotone. This demonstrates model capacity, not identification of channeling.

## Figure 2 — Evidence matrix (`fig2_evidence_matrix`)

**Figure 2. Mechanism evidence matrix with defined status tokens.** Rows are candidate mechanisms and columns report implementation status, observable match, parameter provenance, ability to generate an interior maximum under the tested configuration, and evidence strength. Cell text carries the categorical meaning in addition to colour, and the final column names the decisive missing measurement. Definitions and citations are generated from the committed evidence-matrix and data-dictionary files. The matrix is a provenance and evidence audit, not a numerical ranking or winner scoreboard; statuses are conditional on the implemented model, parameterization, and matched observable.

## Figure 3 — Null-first flow ladder and cross-pressure evaluation (`fig3_kappa_t_ladder`)

**Figure 3. Null-first tests for a rising-flow trajectory.** (a) Numerical reproduction of the published Foster machine/infiltration model curve shows that a dip-and-recovery shape is available without evolving bed resistance; it is a model curve, not measured data. (b) Root-mean-square errors over the same 15–95 s interval compare three constant/static nulls, an empirical time-varying porosity trajectory `Φ(t)`, and a four-parameter cubic. Parameters fitted to the displayed 9-bar flow trace are stated in the labels; imported equilibrium and TDS-sigmoid parameters were fitted to other observables. A flexible temporal null performs comparably, so the result supports a need for temporal flexibility rather than a unique mechanism. (c) Shared-calibration and leave-one-pressure-out RMSE are shown for all 11 pressures; each held-out fit uses the other 10 pressures to estimate the two equilibrium parameters while donor assumptions remain fixed. This is within-rig generalization, not independent validation. (d) The isolated imported swelling branch is evaluated at fixed pressure and predicts throttling opposite to the observed rise. The sign constraint applies only to the tested resistance-only branch and does not exclude swelling in a coupled bed.

## Figure 4 — Shared-porosity composition diagnostic (`fig4_composition_diagnostic`)

**Figure 4. Post-fit shared-porosity composition on the 9-bar flow trace.** Observed flow is compared with the extraction-only implementation identity, the extraction-plus-imported-swelling composition, and the least-squares constant over the common 15–95 s evaluation window. The extraction-only branch reduces by construction to the poroelastic rung; adding the transferred swelling parameterization flattens the trajectory and increases RMSE relative to the constant null. Curves are post-fit or donor-parameterized reconstructions of one trace, not independent predictions. The comparison cannot distinguish parameter-transfer, initial-state, control-regime, or composition-form error.

## Figure 5 — Exploratory N-tube concentration (`fig5_concentration_floortest`)

**Figure 5. Exploratory finite-time flow concentration in an uncoupled N-tube composition.** (a) Effective channel count and maximum single-tube flow share are shown on the physical clock for the baseline fixed-total-flow configuration (`gs=1.1`, `N=400`, zero lateral homogenization); the marked collapse time is stable to the tested timestep refinement. (b) Final effective channel count saturates near its lower bound across `N=100–800` and 4–32 substeps; endpoint saturation is not proof of full trajectory convergence. (c) Final effective channel count is compared across lateral homogenization and flow- versus pressure-control assumptions, showing that concentration is contingent on low lateral exchange and fixed total flow. (d) The closed-form conductance gain scales with the imposed conductance floor, while measured final effective channel count is insensitive over the tested floor sweep. All quantities are deterministic numerical sensitivity results for the implemented start state and regularization. This is an exploratory finite-time concentration result, not a linear-stability theorem or experimental validation.
