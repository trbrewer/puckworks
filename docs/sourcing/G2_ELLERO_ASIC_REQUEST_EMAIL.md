**Subject:** Request for the raw transient-discharge series behind Figs 2–3 of your
2019 SPH espresso-extraction study (ASIC 1993/1997 data)

Dear Prof. Ellero and Dr. Navarini,

I am contributing to an open-source registry of espresso process models, in which
each published model is reproduced as a provenance-tracked, independently-validated
component. Your 2019 SPH study of coffee-bed extraction (PARTICLES 2019, and the
J. Food Eng. 263 journal version) is our reference for the fines-migration
mechanism — mobile fines accumulating in the filter to produce the reversible
transient permeability decay seen in direct/inverse discharge.

To use that mechanism as a *quantitative* validation target (rather than a
qualitative one), we would like the underlying experimental transient-discharge
data that your Fig. 2 overlays as reference markers — i.e. the measured discharge /
Reynolds-number time series from the ASIC colloquium proceedings you cite: Petracco
& Suggi Liverani (15th ASIC, 1993) and Bandini et al. (17th ASIC, 1997), and, if
relevant, Navarini et al. (2009).

Specifically, any of the following would be very helpful, in whatever form exists:
- the raw measured discharge or flow/Reynolds time series (direct and/or inverse
  discharge), ideally machine-readable;
- the applied forcing / pressure schedule and the characteristic time used to
  non-dimensionalize it;
- scans or PDFs of the 1993/1997 ASIC proceedings pages, if the numeric data are
  not separately available.

For full transparency: we have digitized your published Figs 2–4 from the figures
themselves, but we treat those strictly as figure-digitized, qualitative values in
simulation units — they characterize the model, not the coffee — so they cannot
serve as a validation dataset. The measured series behind the Fig. 2 markers is
what would let us test a mass-conserving fines-transport model against experiment.

We would of course cite the original sources and honor any license or reuse
conditions you specify, and we are happy to share back the digitized figures and
our comparison for your review.

Thank you very much for considering this,
