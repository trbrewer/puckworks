## 1. What I understand Paper A and Paper B to be

The repository's current paper-readiness audit recommends **finishing Paper A first**.

### Paper A — extraction kinetics and identifiability

**Core thesis:** fitting single-grind, whole-cup observations cannot reliably separate soluble inventory from extraction rate; fraction-resolved observations restore information about the kinetic rate.

**Current status in the repository:** amber–green; a draft analysis exists, with manuscript conversion, uncertainty, related work, and a reproducibility package still required.

**Natural audiences:** food engineering, food analytics, chemometrics, process measurement, inverse problems, coffee science, and data-driven experimental design.

**Public hook:** **“The cup hides the clock.”** A final cup can look well explained while concealing which process produced it.

### Paper B — flow, evolving-bed models, and mechanism discrimination

**Core thesis:** espresso flow traces support model-class discrimination and expose model-hierarchy limits, but a single trace does not uniquely identify a bed mechanism. The defensible story is not “channeling was proved.” It is that machine-only dynamics, static closures, temporal bed models, and control mode can produce partly degenerate signatures, and that richer perturbations and spatial observables are needed.

**Current status in the repository:** not journal-manuscript-ready. The analysis has valuable conference material, but the repository still flags related-work/novelty positioning and physical lateral coupling as important gaps.

**Natural audiences:** fluid mechanics, transport in deformable porous media, applied mathematics, food engineering, system identification, and coffee science.

**Public hook:** **“One curve, many hidden causes.”** A familiar-looking flow curve can invite a diagnosis that the measurement cannot uniquely support.

### Claim discipline that should govern every submission

For Paper B especially, use verbs such as **“can generate,” “reconstructs,” “is consistent with,” “is required relative to the tested nulls,”** and **“motivates.”** Avoid **“identifies,” “proves,” “is the mechanism,” “channeling instability,”** or an unconditional stability claim unless additional analysis genuinely earns them.

## 2. How the rankings were constructed

I scored venues on six factors, then applied a readiness penalty when the current manuscript does not yet meet the likely bar:

| Factor | Weight | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Relevance to the paper | 30% | Direct topical and methodological match |
| Scientific impact/prestige | 18% | Standing and likely scholarly influence |
| Audience quality | 17% | Probability of reaching the people most able to use, review, cite, or collaborate on the work |
| Public/industry exposure | 15% | Value for the project's stated objective of engaging coffee drinkers and practitioners |
| Timing/actionability | 10% | Whether the call is open, deadline clarity, and realistic preparation time |
| Location/logistics | 10% | Cost, travel burden, and schedule compatibility; Austin, Texas is used only as a rough provisional origin |

Scores are **decision aids, not acceptance probabilities or bibliometric measurements**. A lower-scoring venue can still be the right choice when it reaches a uniquely important audience.
