# Model card: Mo 2023 microCT-SPH percolation

**Paper:** Mo, Johnston, Navarini, Suggi Liverani & Ellero, "Exploring the link between coffee matrix microstructure and flow properties using combined X-ray microtomography and smoothed particle hydrodynamics simulations," arXiv:2305.03911v2 (2023). (No journal DOI on the preprint; check for published version before citing.)
**Stage(s):** flow, packing · **Kind:** calibration
**Status:** card-only

## Scope and mechanism
Real-geometry flow characterization: microCT scans (16.8 µm voxels) of four commercial illy capsule beds (types E/H/M/F, 6.7 g each, ~14.6 × 32.5 mm) are segmented and used as static rigid porous media for SPH percolation simulation on ~708 µm cubic subsamples. The headline physical result is the Darcy–Forchheimer transition: at espresso-relevant pressure gradients (50–400 bar/m, Re 0.84–3.86 on d[4,3]) inertial losses are significant, so apparent Darcy permeability falls with pressure gradient — offered as the explanation for the order-of-magnitude spread in literature k measurements. Secondary result: pre/post-extraction microCT shows a decreasing porosity profile from bottom (outlet) to top (inlet) always develops, attributed to pressure-dependent erosion per the authors' prior SPH erosion model (Mo et al., Phys. Fluids 33, 2021 — a separate paper, not implemented here). No closed-form constitutive model is proposed; the value to us is data plus the Forchheimer functional form with fitted coefficients.

## Governing equations
What would actually be transcribed (the SPH solver itself is not something we would reimplement):

1. Darcy fit, low-∇P regime (their Eq. 1): ∇P = −(µ/k) q, with µ dynamic viscosity, q superficial velocity (discharge), k permeability.
2. Forchheimer fit, high-∇P regime (their Eq. 2): ∇P = −(µ/k) q − (ρ/k₁)|q| q, with ρ fluid density, k₁ the "inertial permeability."
   **Dimensional flag:** as written, Eq. 2 requires [k₁] = m (length), yet Table 2–5 headers give k₁ᶠ in 10⁻⁹ m² and the Fig. 8(b) annotation reads k₁ = 2.17×10⁻¹³ for the same sample the table lists as 4.21×10⁻⁹. Units and value are internally inconsistent by ~10⁴; must be resolved (likely against a standard Forchheimer β = ρ/k₁ form) before the coefficients are used quantitatively.
3. Tortuosity from traced fluid-particle trajectories (their Eq. 3): τ = Σᵢ∫‖dxᵢ‖ / |Σᵢ∫dxᵢ^β|, path length over net displacement along filtration direction β; assumes no recirculation.
4. Tortuosity–porosity power law (their Eq. 4): τ = (1/ε)ⁿ; from their type-H sample ε = 0.17, τ = 1.80 → n = 0.33.

## Calibration and validation offered by the source
- SPH permeability is compared to literature measurements only: their 5.51×10⁻¹³ m² sits between low-overpressure experiments (Gianino 2.3×10⁻¹², King 8.98×10⁻¹³) and ~1-bar experiments (Navarini 0.7–4×10⁻¹³, Corrochano 2.6×10⁻¹⁴–3.4×10⁻¹³), and the pressure-dependence trend matches Navarini qualitatively. No flow measurement on the same capsules was performed, so there is no direct quantitative validation of the simulated k.
- Segmentation is circular for absolute porosity: the greyscale threshold is chosen to reproduce ε = 0.17 from literature (including Cameron [13]). Absolute porosity and everything downstream of it inherit this assumption; the authors acknowledge the resolution cannot separate fines/intragranular pores.
- Forchheimer fits are internal goodness-of-fit ("very good") across 6 samples per powder; the Darcy→Forchheimer transition Re varies between samples, consistent with hydrology literature.
- The authors state plainly that the microstructure–flow link is explored "in a qualitative way" due to computational limits. Do not upgrade: this is a mechanism-and-data paper, not a validated predictive model.
- Post-extraction porosity gradient (bottom > top) is reproduced across medium/dark roast, scan delays of ~0 h, 1 h, 24 h, 3 d — robust as an observation; its erosion explanation is qualitative agreement with their prior model only.

## Assumptions and validity range
- Static, rigid, non-deformable bed: no swelling, erosion, fines migration, or consolidation during flow — the paper's own post-extraction section shows these matter in reality.
- Saturated single-phase isothermal water; silent on wetting/infiltration, degassing, temperature.
- 16.8 µm voxels: fines (~25–50 µm) partially sub-resolution and intragranular pores essentially invisible; solid/void assignment near fines is partial-volume-blurred.
- Subsample size 708 µm ≈ 2 grain diameters for coarse particles: REV questionable; the huge sample-to-sample kᴰ spread (e.g., type E: 0 to 27.6×10⁻¹³ m²; one clogged sample with k = 0) is partly a small-domain effect as well as genuine heterogeneity.
- Periodic boundaries with body-force driving; no basket wall, screen, or filter resistance.
- Capsule (Iperespresso) beds, machine-packed, ε ≈ 0.17: adjacent to but not identical to a tamped 58 mm barista basket.
- Explored regime: ∇P ≈ 8–450 bar/m, Re ≈ 0.84–3.86. Silent below/above; silent on time evolution.

## Overlaps and conflicts
- **Open backlog, flow: Forchheimer/inertial correction — direct hit.** This paper supplies the functional form, the regime map (Re ~ 1–4 at espresso gradients, matching our gusher-regime concern), and 24 fitted (k, k₁) pairs. It is the natural calibration provider for that backlog item once the k₁ units are sorted.
- **brewer2026.lb_reference / lb_taichi (flow, calibration):** complementary and cautionary — our LB twins are Stokes-regime verification tools; Mo shows the espresso operating point is partly outside the linear regime, so LB-derived k should be labelled as low-Re (Darcy) permeability, cf. their kᴰ vs kᶠ gap (~1.4–3×).
- **wadsworth2026.permeability (packing, calibration):** complementary. Mo's beds sit at ε ≈ 0.17 — inside Wadsworth's acknowledged tamped-extrapolation gap — but Mo cannot arbitrate φ_c because absolute porosity is set by circular threshold matching, not measured independently.
- **brewer2026.streamtube (bed_dynamics, runtime):** supporting evidence. Order-of-magnitude kᴰ spread across co-located samples of one bed, growing with coarseness, is direct real-geometry support for lognormal permeability heterogeneity; the fully clogged type-E sample (k = 0) is a real instance of the k→0 atom, though via geometric clogging in a dry rigid bed, not incomplete wetting — it does not discriminate our channeling closure from Foster's unsaturated-flow hypothesis.
- **foster2025.infiltration (infiltration, runtime):** orthogonal (saturated vs wetting), but Mo's SEM-based claim of entrapped gas and incompletely wetted grains independently corroborates the physical premise behind Foster's fine-grind-dip mechanism.
- **bed_dynamics backlog, kappa(t) = kappa0·f(P, eps, E):** the post-extraction bottom-high porosity gradient is the key observational constraint any pressure-dependent erosion/compaction closure must reproduce; the actual erosion model lives in Mo et al. 2021 (Phys. Fluids 33) and Mo et al. 2022 (swelling) — both are candidates for separate intake if that backlog item is picked up.
- **cameron2020:** its ε = 0.17 is used as the segmentation anchor here, so any downstream use of Mo's absolute porosity is not independent of Cameron.
