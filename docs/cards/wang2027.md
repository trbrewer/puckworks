# Model card: wang2027 — high-density-ratio MRT-LBM droplet wetting (liquid sodium)

**Paper/thesis:** Wang, C., Tang, S., Yan, M., Li, Q., Zhang, L., Ma, Z., Lian, Q., Sun, W., Zhu, L., Pan, L. (2027). Dynamic wetting and spreading behaviors of liquid sodium droplets with high density ratio based on an improved MRT-LBM. *Chemical Engineering Science* 337, 124585. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ces.2026.124585
**Stage(s):** infiltration, flow · **Kind:** calibration (method reference only; no runtime candidate)
**Status:** proposed

## Scope and mechanism

Two-dimensional (D2Q9) pseudopotential multiphase lattice Boltzmann model with a multi-relaxation-time (MRT) collision operator, modified forcing/source terms (Huang & Wu 2016 third-order correction), and a Redlich–Kwong equation of state scaled by a factor k to control interface thickness. Purpose: numerically stable simulation of droplet spreading at gas–liquid density ratio ~10⁵ (liquid sodium at heat-pipe temperatures). The paper studies isothermal spreading of single droplets on flat and micro-pillared substrates: contact-line dynamics, dynamic contact angle, spreading exponents, gravity sensitivity, and a Wenzel-spreading-vs-capillary-infiltration transition as pillar height varies. The application domain (liquid sodium, heat-pipe wicks, nuclear) is irrelevant to puckworks; the candidate value is purely the multiphase-LBM machinery for pore-scale wetting, relevant to the unsaturated-flow/incomplete-wetting backlog hypothesis.

## Governing equations

What would actually be implemented if this model class were adopted:

1. **MRT collision in moment space** (Eq. 1): m*(r,t) = m(r,t) − S[m(r,t) − mᵉᑫ(r,t)] + δt(I − S/2)Fₘ(r,t) + SQₘ(r,t), with streaming fᵢ(r + cᵢδt, t + δt) = fᵢ* (Eq. 2). m = Mfᵢ with M the 9×9 Lallemand–Luo orthogonal matrix (Eq. 4); S = diag(s₀, s_e, s_ε, s_j, s_q, s_j, s_q, s_p, s_p) (Eq. 6). Individual relaxation rates sᵢ are **not provided** in the paper.
2. **Equilibrium moments** mᵉᑫ (Eq. 5) — standard D2Q9 form in ρ, u.
3. **Forcing term** Fₘ (Eq. 7) — Guo-style discrete force moments.
4. **Source term** Qₘ (Eqs. 8–9): Qₘ₁ = 3(k₁ + 2k₂)|F|²/(Gψ²c²), Qₘ₇ = k₁(Fₓ² − F_y²)/(Gψ²c²), Qₘ₈ = k₁FₓF_y/(Gψ²c²), with Qₘ₁ = −Qₘ₂ assumed. This is the thermodynamic-consistency tuning of the pseudopotential model; **k₁, k₂ values are not provided**.
5. **Interparticle force** (Eq. 10): F(r,t) = −Gψ(r,t) Σᵢ wᵢψ(r + cᵢδt, t)cᵢ, G = −1.
6. **Interaction potential** (Eq. 11, Yuan–Schaefer): ψ = √(2(p_EOS − ρc_s²)/(Gδₓ²)).
7. **Scaled R–K EOS** (Eq. 12): p = k(ρRT/(1 − bρ) − aρ²/(√T(1 + bρ))). Note: the typeset equation prints "p" where "ρ" is meant in both numerator terms (dimensional check confirms; transcribed as printed, flagged here). a = 0.42748R²T_c^2.5/p_c, b = 0.08664RT_c/p_c; lattice values a = 2/49, b = 2/21, R = 1, scaling k = 0.5.
8. **Wetting boundary conditions**: fictitious-density virtual-particle scheme for flat walls (Eq. 13, prescribes θ via ψᵢ,₁ = ψᵢ,₃ + tan(π/2 − θ)|ψᵢ₊₁,₂ − ψᵢ₋₁,₂|); fluid–solid interaction force for structured walls (Eq. 14) with G_ads = −0.95 and solid indicator function s.
9. **Gravity** (Eq. 15): F_g = (ρ − ρ_ave)g. Macroscopics per Eqs. 16–17 (velocity includes δt F/2 half-force shift).

## Calibration and validation offered by the source

All validation is **verification-grade, not experimental**:

- **Laplace law**: ΔP vs 1/R linear at three reduced temperatures, R² = 0.999748 / 0.997650 / 0.995474. Passes.
- **Maxwell construction**: coexistence densities agree with equal-area construction; "slight deviations" in vapor branch at low T (not quantified).
- **Surface tension realism**: effective σ after unit conversion is **1.20–2.33× the real sodium values** (0.3386 vs 0.1456 N/m at the coldest case). Authors acknowledge this; only the decreasing trend with T is reproduced. So quantitative capillary-pressure magnitudes are wrong by up to ~2.3×.
- **Grid independence**: R_eq/R₀ errors ≤ 1.93%, H_eq/R₀ ≤ 3.03%, θ_eq within 2° across 150²–300² grids (Table 2).
- **Spurious currents**: U_sp,max ≈ 1.03 × 10⁻³ lu/ts, same order as Qin et al. 2018 benchmark.
- **Physics plausibility checks**: early-time spreading slope ≈ 0.5 consistent with inertia–capillary R ~ t^{1/2}; equilibrium contact angles reproduce prescribed θ₀ within a few degrees (42/63/88/107/125/140° vs set 45/65/90/105/120/135°); Bo = 10⁻³–10⁻² so gravity insensitivity is expected and observed.

No comparison to any wetting experiment. No sodium contact-angle data (e.g., Faizal 2023, Bader 1977, both cited) is used quantitatively.

## Assumptions and validity range

- **2D cross-section**; authors explicitly concede 3D pillar arrays differ in capillary suction, pinning positions, and equilibrium spreading scale.
- Isothermal, single droplet, single component, no evaporation/phase change (their own prior work, Tang 2024, handles phase change).
- Sodium property regime: density ratio 10⁵, ν_l ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻⁷ m²/s, σ ≈ 0.1–0.15 N/m — *not* the water/coffee regime (density ratio ~10³ at 90 °C, ν ≈ 3 × 10⁻⁷ m²/s, σ ≈ 0.06 N/m with surfactant-active solutes).
- Prescribed static contact angle as input; no contact-angle hysteresis model; no dynamic wetting closure beyond emergent LBM behavior.
- Effective surface tension miscalibrated by up to 2.33× — fine for regime maps, disqualifying for quantitative capillary-pressure prediction.
- Regular pillar arrays only; silent on random porous media, polydisperse grains, deformable/swelling solids, and dissolved-species transport — all central to coffee.
- Pillar-height transition result (h = 3 lu optimal, h ≥ 5 lu infiltration-dominated) is declared by the authors to be a "relative optimization result under the present physical-property scaling" — not transferable.

## Interface mapping

Inputs consumed: none of the existing contracts map. A pore-scale wetting study would need geometry from packing (brewer2026.pack_generator voxel fields) and fluid properties — neither is plumbed for multiphase.
Outputs produced: nothing that writes to BedState/ShotResultState. At best, offline regime maps (wetting vs infiltration) that could inform the unsaturated-flow hypothesis atom (tubes at k→0) in brewer2026.streamtube.
Couplings: none viable. Adopting this would mean building a second, multiphase LB code path alongside brewer2026.lb_reference/lb_taichi (single-phase TRT), with different collision operator (MRT), EOS machinery, and wetting boundaries. That is a new solver, not an adapter.

## Extractable data

Nothing worth transcribing into puckworks/data/. All results are lattice-unit sodium simulations: Table 1 (unit map), Table 2 (grid independence), Figs. 6–12 (R(t), H(t), θ(t), spreading exponents n(θ₀) = 0.217/0.186/0.128/0.062 for θ₀ = 45–120°), Figs. 14–15 (pillar morphologies). Simulation data "available on request"; no code published. If the multiphase-LBM route is ever pursued, the correct primary references are the cited methods papers (Huang & Wu 2016; Yuan & Schaefer 2006; Lallemand & Luo 2000), which contain the actual sᵢ, k₁, k₂ closures this paper omits.

## Overlaps and conflicts

- **brewer2026.lb_reference / lb_taichi (flow, calibration)**: same numerical family (LB), different physics tier (single-phase saturated Darcy-scale vs multiphase interface capture). Complementary in principle, but this paper does not supply reusable numerics: key parameters (sᵢ, k₁, k₂) unpublished, no code.
- **foster2025.infiltration (infiltration, runtime)**: models the same physical stage (dry-bed wetting) at continuum scale with a sharp-front assumption. wang2027-class pore-scale simulation could in principle test the sharp-front assumption or the incomplete-wetting backlog hypothesis, but this specific paper (sodium, 2D pillars, miscalibrated σ) does not.
- **Backlog "unsaturated flow at fine grinds"**: nearest hook. The paper's spreading-vs-infiltration transition is qualitatively the right physics vocabulary, but the result is declared non-transferable by the authors and the property regime is wrong.
- Competes with nothing; supersedes nothing.
