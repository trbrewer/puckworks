**Status:** proposed (card-only)

## Scope and mechanism
A qualitative magazine overview of coffee from bean to cup: genetics/agronomy (arabica vs robusta), post-harvest processing, roasting chemistry (Maillard, CO₂ evolution, cell-scale pressure buildup), aroma science (GC-olfactometry, dominant odorants), and a lay description of espresso preparation and crema as a polyphasic colloid. There is no mathematical model, no governing equation, and no calibration procedure. The espresso section is folklore-level operating guidance rather than a physics model.

## Governing equations
None. The article contains no equations. The only quasi-quantitative statements are nominal operating conditions and a schematic assertion about the bed operating point:
- Espresso brewed at 92–94 °C, pressurized to ~9 atm, flowing at "around a milliliter a second," yielding ~30 mL in ~30 s.
- Espresso extracts only 60–70 % of the caffeine (vs. near-complete in 4–6 min drip).
- Qualitative operating-point claim: the bed's hydraulic resistance "must be slightly less than the pressure of the steaming-hot extraction water" so flow proceeds at ~1 mL/s. This is a loose verbal statement of a Darcy-type balance, not a usable relation (it conflates a resistance with a pressure and provides no k, geometry, or viscosity).
