# Model card: Lee 2023 two-pathway flow instability

**Paper:** Lee, Smith & Arshad, "Uneven extraction in coffee brewing," Phys. Fluids (AIP formatting; uploaded PDF carries no volume/DOI — believed to be 35, 054110 (2023), DOI 10.1063/5.0138998; verify before registry entry).
**Stage(s):** bed_dynamics, extraction · **Kind:** runtime
**Status:** proposed

## Scope and mechanism
Minimal (0D-per-pathway) model of lateral extraction non-uniformity in an espresso bed, built to test whether a dissolution–flow instability explains the Cameron 2020 fine-grind EY turnover. The bed is split laterally into two equal-volume, equal-area pathways; each has uniform porosity ε_i and dissolved concentration c_i. Positive feedback: extraction raises porosity → Kozeny–Carman permeability → more flow → more extraction; an initial porosity difference δ grows. Key finding: the instability is *always* present in the model; the EY peak vs grind size corresponds not to instability onset but to the fast pathway hitting the solubility ceiling EY_max (saturation), after which its overcompensation stops masking the slow pathway's deficit.

## Governing equations
Flow partition under imposed constant total flow Q (their Eqs. 1–2):

1. Q_i = Q κ_i / (κ_1 + κ_2), i = 1, 2
2. κ_i = ε_i³ / (1 − ε_i)²  (dimensionless Kozeny–Carman)

Extraction–porosity link (their Eq. 3), assuming soluble and insoluble coffee have equal density:

3. EY_i(t) = (ε_i(t) − ε_i(0)) / (1 − ε_i(0))

Dimensional conservation of dissolved and solid coffee per pathway (their Eqs. 5–6):

5. d/dt [ε_i A L c_i / 2] = (D S (c_sat − c_i) / 2λ) θ(EY_max − EY_i) − Q_i c_i
6. d/dt [(1 − ε_i) A L ρ_c / 2] = −(D S (c_sat − c_i) / 2λ) θ(EY_max − EY_i)

Nondimensional form actually integrated (their Eqs. 7–8), with c_i = c_sat C_i and t = (A L ρ_c λ / D S c_sat) τ:

7. dε_i/dτ = (1 − C_i) θ(EY_max − EY_i)
8. α ε_i dC_i/dτ = (1 − C_i)(1 − α C_i) θ(EY_max − EY_i) − [2β κ_i / (κ_1 + κ_2)] C_i

9. α = c_sat / ρ_c
10. β = Q λ / (D S)

Symbols: ε_i porosity of pathway i; c_i / C_i (dimensionless) dissolved-solids concentration; κ_i dimensionless permeability; Q total volumetric flow (= M_shot / ρ_w t_shot, constant); Q_i pathway flow; A, L total bed cross-sectional area and depth; ρ_c coffee solid density; c_sat surface saturation concentration; D diffusivity, λ diffusion length (only the ratio λ/D enters); S total grain surface area in the bed; θ Heaviside function shutting off dissolution at EY_max; δ initial porosity perturbation, ε_{1,2}(0) = ε_0 ± δ; τ dimensionless time.

Auxiliary grinder maps fitted linearly to Cameron 2020 data (their Figs. 2–3):
t_shot(g) = 50.5 − 11.5 g [s]; S(g) = 0.543 − 0.112 g [m²], g = EK43 grind setting.

Single transfer term ⇒ implicitly unimodal PSD (authors note this explicitly). No vertical stratification. Fluid volume assumed unaffected by dissolved solids (following Moroney 2015).

## Calibration and validation offered by the source
Least-squares fit (χ² over EY(g), constrained BFGS) of three parameters (λ/D, δ, EY_max) to the Cameron 2020 EK43 extraction-yield curve, g = 1.1–2.3. The fit reproduces the qualitative peaked EY(g) shape including the fine-grind decline (their Fig. 4). Plausibility checks only, no independent validation: fitted λ/D = 0.125×10⁶ s m⁻¹ vs 0.128 and 0.210×10⁶ s m⁻¹ computed from Moroney 2015 parameters for two coffees; fitted EY_max = 33.8% vs 30.3% derived by Smith & Lee from the Moroney dataset (authors call the fitted value "perhaps a little high"). The authors flag δ = 0.035 as "a little high" (≈20% of ε_0) and state plainly that with the *measured* ρ_c the EY curve plateaus below the critical grind but does not decrease — the decrease appears only with ρ_c doubled. Validation is therefore a fit to the same dataset the model was built to explain, with one deliberately unphysical parameter; there is no out-of-sample test.

## Assumptions and validity range
- Two equal-area, equal-volume pathways; the 50/50 split is fixed, not fitted. Authors propose N pathways / free area split as extensions.
- Uniform ε and c within each pathway; no vertical stratification (silent on depth-dependent transients, early-shot TDS).
- Constant total volumetric flow Q imposed — not pressure-driven; silent on 9-bar constant-pressure operation, pump characteristics, preinfusion.
- Unimodal PSD via single transfer term; authors suspect the unphysical ρ_c compensates for missing fines.
- Soluble and insoluble fractions assumed equal density (needed for Eq. 3).
- δ identical across all grind settings, so the model cannot represent grind-dependent onset of channeling/clogging (authors note this weakens the clogging interpretation).
- Dry-bed wetting ignored (fully saturated from t = 0).
- Validity demonstrated only for Cameron's EK43 g 1.1–2.3, 40 g shots; outside that, extrapolation.
- Fails to produce the decreasing-EY branch at physical ρ_c — the headline behavior is parameter-contingent.

## Interface mapping
Inputs consumed: BedState (porosity → ε_0, area, depth; dose via M_shot), GrindState.setting via the grinder-specific t_shot(g) and S(g) linear maps (EK43-only; adapters needed for any other grinder). MachineState: **not consumed** — flow is imposed constant; a pressure-driven variant would need Q_i derived from Darcy with MachineState.P_of_t and dimensional κ (adapter + reformulation, since κ here is dimensionless and unnormalized).
Outputs produced: ShotResultState.EY_pct (overall and per-pathway), traces of ε_i(τ), C_i(τ).
Coupling: runtime bed_dynamics component; naturally expressed as an ε-dependent permeability closure κ_i(ε_i(t)) that could be grafted onto the existing streamtube ensemble rather than kept as a standalone two-tube model.

## Extractable data
Nothing new: all experimental data is Cameron 2020, already held in-registry. Worth recording: the linear-fit coefficients (a₀ = 50.5 s, a₁ = −11.5 s; b₀ = 0.543 m², b₁ = −0.112 m²) and the fitted-parameter table above, as a reproduction target. No code or data repository is cited in the paper.

## Errata (paper typos, resolved by reviewer with the paper in evidence)
- **Table I α = 3.76 is a reciprocal typo.** Eq. 9 (α = c_sat/ρ_c) is correct —
  confirmed by independent re-derivation of the nondimensional Eq. 8 from the
  dimensional Eqs. 5–6. The tabulated 3.76 = ρ_c/c_sat (the inverse). Use
  α = c_sat/ρ_c: **0.2662** for the imposed ρ_c = 798 (headline peaked run),
  **0.5323** for the physical ρ_c = 399 (negative-result run). ρ_c enters the
  dynamics ONLY through α; the dimensionless shot time τ_shot below is
  ρ_c-independent.
- **Overall EY = solid-depletion, mass-weighted across the two pathways:**
  EY_tot = [(ε₁−ε₁₀)+(ε₂−ε₂₀)] / [(1−ε₁₀)+(1−ε₂₀)], evaluated at τ_shot(g).
- **Shot-time nondimensionalization** (ρ_c cancels via A·L·ρ_c = M_dose/(1−ε₀)):
  τ_shot(g) = t_shot(g) · (D/λ) · S(g) · c_sat · (1−ε₀) / M_dose, with
  D/λ = 8.0×10⁻⁶ m/s, M_dose = 0.020 kg, ε₀ = 0.173. Cross-check:
  τ_shot(1.1) = 1.12, τ_shot(2.3) = 0.48 (match the Figs. 6–7 time axes).
- **Permeability κ = ε³/(1−ε)²** (Kozeny–Carman, as in Governing equations);
  the (1−ε²) reading of the scan is wrong — it numerically eliminates the
  interior EY(g) peak.

## Overlaps and conflicts
- **brewer2026.streamtube (competes/complements):** streamtube posits *static* lognormal permeability heterogeneity; this paper posits *dynamic* heterogeneity growth via the extraction→ε→κ feedback from an arbitrarily small seed (δ). These are distinguishable: streamtube needs finite initial σ, Lee needs only the feedback. The natural synthesis is adding the ε(t)-dependent κ closure per tube to streamtube (an N-tube generalization the authors themselves suggest). Note their key reinterpretation — the EY peak is a *saturation* effect, not instability onset — is a direct alternative to our channeling closure σ(φ₁).
- **cameron2020.extraction_bdf (complements/competes):** uses Cameron's data; its lateral two-pathway mechanism is an explicit alternative to Cameron's imposed reduced-participating-area explanation. Far lower extraction fidelity (no fines/boulders, no intragrain diffusion).
- **foster2025.infiltration (competing hypothesis):** this is now the *third* mechanism on the table for the fine-grind EY dip — (a) channeling/static heterogeneity (streamtube), (b) incomplete wetting (Foster), (c) dissolution–flow instability + saturation (Lee). Discriminators: per-pathway EY (Lee predicts one region fully depleted → bitterness signature), first-drip timing (Foster), σ vs φ₁ (streamtube).
- **Backlog hit:** "bed_dynamics: pressure/history-dependent permeability κ(t)" — this paper supplies the extraction-driven branch of that closure (κ grows with dissolution), complementary to compaction/swelling (κ falls with pressure/strain). Both belong in the same κ(t) = κ₀ f(P, ε, E) framework.

## Implementation estimate
Small: 2 coupled ODE pairs, Heaviside cutoff, RK45; fits reproducible with scipy. Gate design: (1) reproduce their Fig. 4 best-fit EY(g) curve and Table I fitted parameters from Cameron data; (2) confirm their negative result — plateau, not decline, at physical ρ_c = 399 — as a documented failure mode; (3) N-tube extension inside streamtube is the real payoff (M effort, separate work item). Dependencies: none beyond data already held.

VERDICT: implement-later — the extraction→porosity→permeability feedback is a cheap, discriminable third hypothesis for the fine-grind dip and the missing dynamic branch of the backlog κ(t) closure, but the published model needs an unphysical ρ_c to show the headline trend and brings no new data, so it earns a slot only as an N-tube closure grafted onto streamtube, not as a standalone component — effort S.
