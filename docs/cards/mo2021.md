# Model card: Mo 2021 SDPD Mohr-Coulomb erosion

**Paper:** Mo, C., Johnston, R., Navarini, L. & Ellero, M., "Modeling the effect of flow-induced mechanical erosion during coffee filtration," Physics of Fluids 33 (2021). DOI 10.1063/5.0059707 (accepted-manuscript copy, dated 4 Aug 2021). Same group/lineage as ellero2019 (Illycaffè-funded mesoscopic espresso programme); this is the "prior SPH erosion model" cited by mo2023 as the explanation for its post-extraction porosity gradients.
**Stage(s):** bed_dynamics, flow · **Kind:** calibration (mesoscopic reference simulation; runtime coupling would be forced)
**Status:** card-only

## Scope and mechanism
2D smoothed dissipative particle dynamics (SDPD) simulation of mechanical (not chemical/dissolution) erosion of a model porous coffee bed. Grains are clusters of SDPD particles bonded by harmonic springs; surface particles erode probabilistically when the most-stretched bond exceeds a threshold, which the authors show is macroscopically equivalent to a shear-erosion law with a Mohr-Coulomb (pressure-dependent) yield threshold: compressive normal stress suppresses erosion, tensile enhances it. Physical justification: incompletely wetted, gas-entrapped grains remain brittle early in the shot, so brittle-failure (pressure-dependent) erosion is more appropriate than pure shear erosion. Headline results, all at prescribed constant flux through a 225-disk random 2D medium: (i) pressure-dependent erosion generates medium-scale heterogeneity in the filtration direction — the low-pressure (outlet) end erodes faster, producing a porosity gradient — controlled by the internal-friction angle ψ; (ii) transverse heterogeneity (channelization) is enhanced by the cohesion parameter c and suppressed by ψ. Everything is dimensionless; no SI anchoring anywhere.

## Governing equations
The SDPD solver (their Eqs. 1–4) would only be transcribed if the framework were reimplemented; the erosion model (Eqs. 5–12, 16–17) is the paper's content.

SDPD pairwise forces (their Eq. 1): conservative F^C_ij = (P_i/d_i² + P_j/d_j²) F_ij r_ij; dissipative F^D_ij = −γ_ij [v_ij + (e_ij·v_ij) e_ij]; rotational F^R_ij = −γ_ij (r_ij/2) × (ψ_i + ψ_j); stochastic F̃_ij = σ_ij (dW^s_ij + ⅓tr[dW_ij]1)·e_ij/dt. Here r_ij = r_i − r_j, v_ij = v_i − v_j, e_ij = r_ij/r_ij; d_i = Σ_j W_ij is number density with kernel W of cutoff r_c defining F_ij via ∇_i W_ij = −r_ij F_ij; pressure from EoS P_i = P_0(d_i/d_0)^ν − P_b; ψ_i is particle spin (angular-momentum-conserving SDPD variant). Force amplitudes (Eq. 2): γ_ij = (20η/7) F_ij/(d_i d_j), σ_ij = 2√(k_B T γ_ij), with η fluid dynamic viscosity. Equations of motion (Eq. 3) integrated with velocity-Verlet; 2D Lucy kernel (Eq. 4): W(r) = (5/πr_c²)(1 + 3r/r_c)(1 − r/r_c)³.

Erosion micro-model (Eqs. 5–9). A surface particle under fluid force F, held by springs of stiffness k with unit direction vectors l̂_i to interior particles, displaces by d = (Σ_i l̂_i l̂_i)^{-1}·F/k (Eq. 5); each spring stretches Δl_i = l̂_i·d (Eq. 6). The most-stretched spring is eligible to break with probability p_i = min{p_0(Δl_i − Δl_c), 1} (Eq. 7), Δl_c a global stretch threshold, checked every N_freq timesteps; expected spring lifetime T_i = N_freq Δt/p_i (Eq. 8); mass erosion rate k_er = m/Σ_{i∈S_b} T_i over the break sequence S_b (Eq. 9). A particle detaches (joins the fluid) when all its bonds are broken; deposition is not modeled.

Effective macroscopic law (Eqs. 11–12), the form a continuum closure would take: k_er = max{C_E(τ − τ_c), 0} with τ_c = σ tanψ + c, where τ is local shear stress, σ applied normal stress (compression negative in their force plots), ψ the angle of internal friction, c cohesion. C_E has no closed form; estimated by C′_E ≡ m p_0 (Δx)^{d−1}/(k N_freq Δt) (Eq. 12), Δx average particle spacing, d spatial dimension. c is set implicitly by Δl_c (mapping only available numerically, Fig. 7); ψ is set by scaling the conservative force on well-supported surface particles by α (Eq. 16), with α = tanψ (Eq. 17). Without the α modification ψ = π/4 always; and at small σ with larger Δx the failure envelope slope drops below 1, i.e. the model follows Mohr-Coulomb only approximately (authors' own caveat).

Validation-configuration equations (Eqs. 13–15, flat-wall shear erosion at Δl_c = 0): dL/dt = η v_x k_w/L, solution L = √(2 k_w v_x η t + L_0²), with wall erosion rate k_w = Δx k_er/m; L fluid-layer height, v_x wall speed.

## Calibration and validation offered by the source
Verification-only, model-vs-model throughout; zero experimental comparison.
- Flat-wall shear-erosion test (Sec. II C): fitted k_w = 3.1 × 10⁻⁴ ± 0.1 × 10⁻⁴ vs the value 3.3 × 10⁻⁴ predicted by the model's own Eqs. (5)–(9) + (15), across v_x = 1.0–2.5, with L(t) well fit by Eq. (14). This is internal consistency of the implementation with its own analytic reduction, not validation against any material.
- Pore-size-distribution evolution (Weibull fits, Figs. 10–11) reported as "in good agreement with the simulations of Chiu et al." (JFM 2020, a shear-erosion boundary-integral study) — simulation-vs-simulation.
- The erosion-rate surface (Figs. 2–3) demonstrates the intended Mohr-Coulomb + shear-erosion behavior (linear τ_c(σ), slope 1 at large σ) — characterization, not validation.
- No coffee bed, flow trace, permeability, or porosity measurement is compared. The paper motivates the mechanism from literature SEM/wetting observations (Mateus 2007 gas entrapment; Pittia 2001 roasting embrittlement) but validates none of its erosion predictions.

## Assumptions and validity range
- 2D, periodic laterally; disks, not real grain shapes; grains fully rigid until particle detachment (no consolidation, swelling, or ductile transition — the wetting-induced brittleness assumption is argued to hold only in the early shot, ~first 5 s).
- Prescribed constant flux only. Constant-ΔP is deliberately not simulated (citing Jäger: fixed ΔP ends in either total clogging or total erosion); espresso machines are closer to fixed-ΔP, so the operating regime of an actual shot is exactly the case the paper avoids.
- Eroded particles vanish into the fluid: no deposition, no fines re-trapping, no filter — the opposite limit from ellero2019, where all impedance came from filter-trapped fines.
- Low Re (0.04) vs mo2023's espresso estimate Re ≈ 0.84–3.86; no inertial/Forchheimer regime.
- Dimensionless: c, ψ, C_E for actual wetted coffee are unknown; the authors' proposed control knobs (temperature, water content) are speculative.
- Mohr-Coulomb followed only approximately at small normal stress (slope < 1); erosion threshold mapping c(Δl_c) is numerical, not closed-form.
- Silent on: extraction/solutes, temperature, unsaturated flow, 3D effects, tamped-bed stress state, basket/screen boundary.

## Interface mapping
Inputs consumed: none of the v0.1 contracts — dimensionless grain-scale geometry and prescribed flux. Outputs produced: nothing landing in BedState/ShotResultState without full re-dimensionalization and a 2D→3D credibility argument.
Couplings: offline calibration at best. The plausible chain is the effective law (Eq. 11) as a bed_dynamics closure candidate: a future κ(t) component of the form dε/dt ∝ C_E max{τ(z,t) − σ(z,t)tanψ − c, 0} with τ, σ from the local flow/pressure solution — i.e., this paper supplies a *functional form* with a pressure-suppression term, not parameters. Adapters needed: SI anchoring of (C_E, c, ψ), 2D→3D, and a local-stress estimator from Darcy-scale fields (τ_ref-style pore-scale reconstruction). Runtime SDPD coupling is the mega-model failure mode and is not proposed.

## Extractable data
Nothing transcribable of registry value: all figures are in simulation units under nominal parameters and characterize the model, not coffee. The qualitative results worth recording in prose (already in this card): erosion-direction asymmetry (outlet erodes faster under constant downward flux, Figs. 8–9), c* enhances / tanψ suppresses channelization (Figs. 13–17), pore-size mean grows steadily with ε while variance blows up only at high ε (Figs. 11, 15, 18). Data "available from the corresponding author upon reasonable request"; no code released. The follow-on microCT programme announced in the outlook materialized as mo2023 — already carded, and its Tables 2–5 are the data this paper lacks.

## Overlaps and conflicts
- **mo2023 (carded, calibration-provider):** completes the pair. mo2023's post-extraction microCT porosity gradient (bottom/outlet more porous than top/inlet, robust across roasts and scan delays) is the observational constraint; this paper is the proposed mechanism, and the sign matches: high inlet pressure consolidates and suppresses erosion, outlet erodes freely. That is qualitative agreement between two products of the same group — supportive, not independent validation.
- **Open backlog, bed_dynamics κ(t) = κ_0·f(P, ε, E):** erosion enters with the *right sign* for the rising-flow residual (mass removal → ε↑ → k↑ → flow↑ at fixed ΔP), in direct contrast to mo2023_2 swelling, which predicts falling flow at fixed ΔP. The registry now holds both signed mechanisms from the same group; a discrimination experiment (flow-trace sign + reversibility: erosion irreversible, filter-fines reversible per ellero2019, swelling irreversible-negative) is designable on paper. Caveat: this paper never runs the fixed-ΔP case where the sign claim would bite.
- **ellero2019 (carded, skip):** sibling mesoscopic solver, same programme, same verdict logic — 2D, dimensionless, no data, no code. Mechanistically opposite bed_dynamics limit (grain mass loss here vs fines accumulation there); both are citations for mechanism hypotheses, not implementables.
- **brewer2026.streamtube (bed_dynamics, runtime):** the channelization results are grain-scale mechanistic support for *time-broadening* tube heterogeneity (larger pores erode faster → variance of pore sizes grows, Fig. 15) — i.e., σ need not be static. Also a warning: channelization strength depends on material yield properties (c, ψ) nobody has measured for wetted coffee.
- **brewer2026.pack_generator / lb_reference:** a pack-generator geometry plus an LB solver with a stress-triggered mass-removal rule would reproduce this paper's physics in our own verified stack at lower cost than reimplementing SDPD, if the mechanism is ever needed quantitatively.
- **cameron2020.extraction_bdf / foster2025.infiltration:** disjoint (no solutes; saturated from t = 0), though the incomplete-wetting premise (gas-entrapped brittle grains) independently echoes the physical picture behind Foster's fine-grind-dip hypothesis and the incomplete-wetting backlog item.

## Implementation estimate
Reimplementing the SDPD erosion framework: L (angular-momentum-conserving SDPD, bonded-cluster solids, probabilistic bond breaking, Gabriel-graph pore metrology), for output that would remain dimensionless and unvalidated against coffee. Not proposed. The cheap alternative if the mechanism is scheduled: (1) adopt Eq. No gate is runnable against this paper itself — there is nothing quantitative to hit.
