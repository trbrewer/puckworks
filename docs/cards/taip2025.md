# Model card: Taip 2025 infusion FOPTD + PI-control study

**Paper/thesis:** Taip, F.S., Abdul Ghani, N.H., Othman, N., "Empirical
modelling, simulation and control of coffee brewing." *Food Research* 9(2),
32–39 (April 2025). DOI 10.26656/fr.2017.9(2).030 (open access).
**Stage(s):** observables (nominal, poor fit) · **Kind:** calibration
**Status:** card-only (skip)

## Scope and mechanism
Immersion ("infusion") brewing of 500 µm Arabica at 80/85/90 °C, fixed dose and
coffee-to-water ratio. Caffeine and total "coffee" concentration are measured
vs. time; the concentration-vs-time response is then fit as a black-box
first-order-plus-time-delay (FOPTD) transfer function with temperature as the
input, and that transfer function is fed to a closed-loop PI controller tuned by
four rule sets (Ziegler–Nichols, IMC, Chen et al. fractional-order, Gude–Kahoraho
fractional-order), compared on IAE and gain/phase margin. There is no bed,
pressure, flow, or transport physics: the "model" is a linear SISO identification
of a steeping curve, and the bulk of the paper is a process-control tuning
exercise. Not espresso.

## Governing equations
**(Eq. 1)** FOPTD plant transfer function:
  G(s) = K·e^(−θs) / (τs + 1)
K = process gain, τ = time constant (s), θ = time delay/dead time (s), s = Laplace
variable.

**(Eq. 2)** Process gain:
  K = ΔOutput/ΔInput = ΔConcentration/ΔTemperature   (units g L⁻¹ °C⁻¹)

**(Eq. 3)** Time constant, Method 1 (63.2% response):
  τ = τ_63.2% − θ

**(Eqs. 4–5)** Time delay and time constant, Method 2 (Sundaresan–Krishnaswamy
1978, using response times t₁, t₂ at 35.3% and 85.3%):
  θ = 1.3·t₁ − 0.29·t₂
  τ = 0.67·(t₂ − t₁)

**(Eq. 6)** Fit metric:
  RMSE = √[ (1/N)·Σ(x_actual − x_model)² ]

**(Eq. 16)** Reported caffeine-vs-temperature correlation (x = °C, y = caffeine
conc.):
  y = 33.981·e^(0.0201x),  R² = 0.9371

Eqs. 7–15 are the generic PI/fractional-PI tuning rules for the four controllers
(K_p, τ_i, K_c formulas of ZN, IMC, Chen et al. 2008, Gude–Kahoraho 2009). They
contain no coffee physics and would not be implemented in puckworks; transcription
omitted as out of scope.

## Calibration and validation offered by the source
Effectively none usable. "Validation" is in-sample RMSE of the FOPTD fit against
the same steeping curves, with no held-out data and no external comparison. The
RMSE values (~20.6–21.4 g L⁻¹) are of the **same magnitude as the entire
concentration signal** (data top out near 30 g L⁻¹), so the "best-fitting" model
does not actually track the data — this is a non-fit dressed as a fit. Controller
performance (Table 3–4: IAE 64.67 best, phase/gain margins) is a simulation of the
identified plant, i.e. it validates the tuning arithmetic, not any coffee outcome.

Two hard data-quality problems:
- **The headline caffeine value, 210.58 g L⁻¹, is physically impossible.** Brewed
  coffee caffeine is ~0.2–2 g L⁻¹; 210 g L⁻¹ would exceed a saturated caffeine
  solution. Almost certainly a units/reporting error (mg L⁻¹? mislabel). Unusable
  as data.
- **The concentration trend contradicts itself.** Total coffee concentration
  *decreases* with temperature (80 °C highest at 30.94 g L⁻¹, 90 °C lowest at
  21.45 g L⁻¹), the opposite of the caffeine claim and of standard extraction
  physics; the authors attribute it to uncontrolled volatile loss / open
  environment, i.e. an apparatus artifact they could not control.

## Assumptions and validity range
- Immersion brewing only — no pressure, no puck, no flow, no percolation. None of
  the registry's espresso stages (packing/machine/infiltration/flow/bed_dynamics)
  has an analogue here.
- Black-box SISO identification: temperature step → concentration. No mechanism,
  no transport, no composition beyond lumped caffeine / total solids. Zero transfer
  to other beans, doses, ratios, grinds, or to espresso.
- Only three temperatures (80/85/90 °C), single bean/ratio/grind; no replication
  reported. Equilibrium reached ~210 s.
- Silent on: pressure brewing, flow, spatial non-uniformity/channeling, solubility
  ceilings, multi-species chemistry, and the infiltration transient.
- Failure modes: the fit itself (RMSE ≈ signal); the impossible caffeine value;
  the internally contradictory temperature trend; controller results with no
  physical referent.

## Interface mapping
Inputs consumed: none from the shot chain — temperature is a protocol scalar, not
a MachineState field, and there is no pressure/flow input. Outputs produced: none
into any contract; at best a ShotResultState.traces-shaped concentration-vs-time
curve for immersion, which the chain does not consume. Couplings: none. The PI
controller regulates caffeine concentration by manipulating temperature — a
control loop with no counterpart in puckworks' machine model (which is a physical
pump/headspace, not a concentration setpoint regulator). No adapter warranted.
