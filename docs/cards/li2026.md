# Model card: li2026 — shape-dependent granular column collapse (SH-reconstructed concave–convex particles, polyhedral DEM)

**Paper/thesis:** Y. Li, C. Zhai, D. Wei, R. Fuentes, "The role of concave–convex particle morphology in granular materials collapse via spherical harmonic reconstruction," *Powder Technology* 484 (2026) 122849. DOI 10.1016/j.powtec.2026.122849. Open access (CC BY).
**Stage(s):** packing (nearest fit; see Interface mapping — no stage actually matches) · **Kind:** calibration (nominally; nothing consumable)
**Status:** card-only

## Scope and mechanism

Gravity-driven collapse of a released rectangular column of dry, cohesionless, mono-sized (d ≈ 2 mm) particles with realistic concave–convex morphology, generated by Spherical Harmonic (SH) reconstruction from two fractal descriptors (Df, D2) and simulated in polyhedral DEM (Rocky), calibrated against the authors' own 3D-printed-particle experiments. Deliverables: a shape descriptor Rr collapsing (Df, D2) to one scalar; an empirical runout scaling L*(a, Rr) (their Eq. 8); and comparative internal diagnostics (friction-mobilization index I_AM, energy partition). Domain is geotechnical (rock avalanche / landslide runout). No fluid, no cohesion, no compaction under external stress, no fine-particle physics.

## Governing equations

Only Eq. (8) is a "model" one could implement; the rest are definitions/diagnostics. Transcribed as printed.

- **Eq. (1)** — relative roughness: Rr = sqrt( Σ_{n=1}^{n_max} [ D2 · (n/2)^{2Df − 6} ]² ). Df: fractal dimension (surface texture scale-decay); D2: amplitude of the n = 2 (shape-scale) SH descriptor; n: SH degree. Integrates how the surface deviates globally from its c0-determined sphere.
- **Eqs. (2)–(4)** — classical collapse scalings: L* = (r∞ − r0)/r0 = Cα·a for a ≤ a_CL; L* = Cβ·a^{c_EL} for a ≥ a_CL; H* = h∞/r0 = Cγ·a^{c_EH} for a ≥ a_CH. a = H0/L0 initial column aspect ratio; r0, r∞ initial/final run-out extent; h∞ final height; a_CL, a_CH critical aspect ratios; Cα, Cβ, Cγ, c_EL, c_EH empirical constants.
- **Eq. (5)–(6)** — shape-generalized runout: constants become functions of Rr, form (a_i/e^{b_i·Rr} + c_i), linear branch (i = 1) below a_CL, power branch (i = 2, exponent fixed at 0.7) above.
- **Eq. (8)** — final fitted model:
  L* = (0.3192/e^{3.8581·Rr} + 0.6806)·a for a < a_CL;
  L* = (1.3634/e^{0.6707·Rr})·a^{0.7} for a ≥ a_CL;
  a_CL = [ (1.3634/e^{0.6707·Rr}) / (0.3192/e^{3.8581·Rr} + 0.6806) ]^{10/3} (Eq. 7 continuity condition).
- **Eqs. (9)–(10)** — interlocking diagnostic: I_AM = [F_T,ave(i)/(μp·F_N,ave(i)) − F_T,ave(sph)/(μp·F_N,ave(sph))] / [F_T,ave(sph)/(μp·F_N,ave(sph))], average tangential/normal contact forces normalized to the spherical reference; response-based, not a pure geometric descriptor (authors' own caveat).
- **Eqs. (11)–(14)** — energy balance E0 = EP + EKT + EKR + EDA with EP = Σ m_i g h_i, EKT = Σ ½ m_i v_i², EKR = Σ ½ I_i ω_i²; EDA (dissipation) is the residual and includes viscous contact damping, which at ε = 0.3 is a substantial fraction — the authors explicitly say dissipation magnitudes are comparative, not material properties.

Nothing simplified away by us.

## Calibration and validation offered by the source

Layered and partly circular, which the authors are unusually candid about: (i) DEM contact parameters were tuned by trial and error **to** the experimental L*, with H* and deposit morphology as secondary checks (Fig. 6, trend-level agreement, no error metrics printed) — so L* agreement is calibration, not validation; (ii) parameters are declared phenomenological, and all internal quantities (velocity fields, I_AM, energy) are interpreted only comparatively across shapes; (iii) Eq. 8 is fitted to their own simulations (branch R² 0.94–0.98) and compared against their own experiments (Fig. 12, visual agreement); the abstract itself states further validation is required beyond the tested morphology range and collapse conditions. The 320-face simplification is justified only by macroscopic agreement, not contact-level convergence. Per-shape scaling regressions report R² > 0.99 (Table 3). Experimental side is solid as an experiment: 3D-printed particles CT-verified within 5 % of input geometry, 2000 fps imaging, servo door at 1.6 m/s.

## Assumptions and validity range

- Rr ∈ [0, 0.378] only; d ≈ 2 mm mono-sized; dry, cohesionless, rigid particles; rectangular channel (12 mm ≈ 6d wide — wall-affected quasi-2D); a up to ~6; explicitly not validated outside this envelope (authors' stated limitation).
- Gravity-driven free collapse after door release — no external confining stress, no compaction, no fluid phase, no fines, no size distribution.
- Failure modes for our purposes: coffee grounds are 1–2 orders finer (cohesion/van der Waals and moisture dominate at 20–400 µm, absent here), polydisperse, and never undergo unconfined column collapse in an espresso workflow; tamping is stress-driven consolidation, the opposite loading path. Silent on: interstitial fluid, humidity, cohesion, particle breakage, wall geometry other than the channel.
- I_AM is normalized to a spherical reference and depends on μ, packing state, and system size — not transferable as a number.

## Interface mapping

Inputs consumed: none map. GrindState carries no shape descriptors (Rr, sphericity, convexity, roundness are not fields), and no registry stage models dry mound/column collapse (dosing/distribution before tamping is the only loosely analogous moment, and no such backlog item exists).
Outputs produced: none reach any contract. BedState.porosity is not informed — packing density here is an initial condition (0.62–0.67, rainfall-filled), not a shape→porosity model.
Couplings: none viable, runtime or calibration. Even as a future dosing/distribution prior, Eq. 8 predicts runout of a released 50 mm column of 2 mm cohesionless grains, not the settling of cohesive powder in a 58 mm basket.

## Overlaps and conflicts

- **wadsworth2026.permeability (packing, calibration):** conceptual cousin, no contest — both parameterize grain angularity/roughness scalars (Wadsworth ⟨R⟩ with exp(α⟨R⟩) into k; here Rr into runout). Endpoints are disjoint (permeability vs. collapse runout); nothing to reconcile. If GrindState ever grows a shape field, the two descriptors would need a mapping — noted, not actionable.
- **brewer2026.pack_generator (packing, calibration):** no overlap; the pack generator is Boolean overlapping spheres and takes no morphology beyond radius; this paper offers no porosity–shape closure to feed it.
- **abedi2025 (packing, card-only skip):** same neighborhood of "dry-powder mechanics that doesn't connect" — abedi at least measured coffee; this paper's material is UV-cured polymer at 2 mm.
- **Open backlog:** matches no item (checked grind PSD, bed_dynamics compaction, unsaturated flow, tamping — none opened). Competes with and supersedes nothing.
