# Model card: ICMS Modelling Camp 2016 — filter-coffee lumped extraction + inclined Darcy bed

**Paper/thesis:** Anonymous team, "Brewing Filter Coffee: Mathematical Model of Coffee Extraction," final presentation, Modelling Camp, ICMS Edinburgh, 24 March 2016. Slide deck only; no authors named on slides, no paper, no DOI. (Citekey icms2016 in lieu of firstauthorYEAR — no first author is identifiable from the artifact.)
**Stage(s):** extraction, flow · **Kind:** calibration at most (nothing here could be coupled at runtime; see below)
**Status:** card-only

## Scope and mechanism
Workshop-grade model of drip/filter coffee (gravity-fed cone filter, not espresso) in two loosely joined pieces. (1) A lumped two-species bilinear extraction kinetics: dissolved-coffee concentration C_c and granule-bound concentration C_g exchange mass at a rate proportional to (C_g − Gλ)(S − C_c), i.e. driving force = remaining extractable solids × distance from solubility ceiling; solved in closed form without advection, then re-derived as a depth-averaged ("mean-field") ODE pair with a crude advective loss term. (2) A Darcy flow model of a thin coffee bed of profile h(x) under a hydrostatic water head H, including a rotated (inclined-filter wall) variant; pressure and vertical seepage velocity are given in closed form and used to argue extraction is stronger at the top of the filter and that shallower inclination raises cup concentration. The two pieces are combined only through the illustrative advection solution; there is no consistent coupled PDE statement.

## Governing equations
Slides carry no equation numbers; equations are transcribed as displayed, in slide order.

Variables (slide "Variables"): C_c := m_c/(Vθ), C_g := m_g/(V(1−θ)), with m_c, m_g masses of dissolved coffee and of coffee in granules in a control volume V; θ = bed porosity.

1. Transport of dissolved coffee (slide "Basic Equations"):
   dC_c/dt = α(1−θ)(C_g − Gλ)(S − C_c) − (v_w · ∇C_c)
   G = starting granule concentration; S = maximum concentration of dissolved coffee (solubility); α = extraction rate; v_w = water velocity. λ is used throughout as an unextractable-fraction parameter (residual C_g → Gλ) but is **never defined on any slide**. Note the internal inconsistency, transcribed as written: the left side is written d/dt (total derivative) while the right side carries an explicit Eulerian advection term — the equation mixes material- and fixed-frame forms.
2. Granule balance: dC_g/dt = −θα(C_g − Gλ)(S − C_c).
3. Stated conservation: d/dt[θC_c + (1−θ)C_g] = 0 ⟹ θC_c + (1−θ)C_g = (1−θ)G. As written this is inconsistent with Eq. 1: adding θ×(1) + (1−θ)×(2) leaves the advective term −θ(v_w·∇C_c), so the invariant holds only in the no-advection case. Not corrected on the slides; flagged here.
4. Dimensionless no-advection system, with C̃_c := C_c/S, C̃_g := C_g/G, t̃ = t/T, B = G/S (T never specified):
   dC̃_c/dt̃ = B(1−θ)G(C̃_g − λ)(1 − C̃_c);  dC̃_g/dt̃ = −θ(C̃_g − λ)(1 − C̃_c).
   Dimensional hazard, transcribed as written: the first RHS carries a bare factor G (dimensional) inside an otherwise dimensionless equation — either a typo for a dimensionless group or an inconsistent scaling; the closed-form solution below drops it, suggesting typo.
5. Closed-form solution (slide "Results for the concentrations"):
   C̃_c = [B(1−θ)/θ](1 − C̃_g);
   C̃_g = [λθ + (1−λ)(θ − B(1−θ))e^(−j̃t̃)] / [θ + (1−λ)(1−θ)Be^(−j̃t̃)], with j̃ = θ − (1−λ)B(1−θ).
6. Darcy flow (slide "Flow Through the Coffee-Bed"): q = −(k/μ)∇P, bed occupying 0 ≤ y ≤ h(x) under water to height H; coordinate map x = Lu, y = h(u)v.
7. Pressure and vertical seepage velocity (slide "Pressure-Velocity"):
   P = ρ_w g y (H/h(x) − 1) + P_0;  v_y = −(κ/(θμ)) ρ_w g (H/h(x) − 1).
   Symbols: ρ_w water density, g gravity, P_0 reference (atmospheric) pressure, μ viscosity; permeability is written k in Eq. 6 and κ in Eq. 7 (symbol collision, uncorrected on slides).
8. Rotated (inclined-wall) pressure (slide "Rotating the Problem"): P = ρ_w g h⁻¹ y′ (H − x′ sin φ − h(x′) cos φ), with (x′, y′) wall-aligned coordinates and φ the filter inclination angle. The h⁻¹ prefactor is dimensionally suspect against Eq. 7 (renders P independent of position through h in a different way); transcribed as written.
9. Depth averaging (slides "Mean-field Approximation" / "Average"): C̃_c = (1/h)∫₀ʰ C_c dz etc.; advective divergence averaged as (1/h)∫₀ʰ ∇·(v_w C_c) dz = v_w(C_c(h) − C_c(0)) = −v_w C̃_c — the last equality silently assumes C_c(h) = 0 (fresh water at inlet) and C_c(0) = C̃_c (outlet at the mean), neither stated. Mean-field closure (1/h)∫ f(C_c,C_g) dz ≈ f(C̃_c, C̃_v), where C̃_v is undefined (presumably a typo for C̃_g). Averaged system:
   ∂Ĉ_c/∂t − v_w Ĉ_c = α(1−θ)(Ĉ_g − Gλ)(S − Ĉ_c);  ∂Ĉ_g/∂t = −αθ(Ĉ_g − Gλ)(S − Ĉ_c).

## Calibration and validation offered by the source
None. No experimental data of any kind. The only outward-facing check is qualitative placement of four simulated brews (h = 0.7–1 cm) on the SCA brewing control chart (slide "Brewing Contral Chart Comparison" [sic]), from which the team reads off 0.8 < h < 1 cm as the "ideal" band. That chart placement depends entirely on the unreported parameter values, so it is not reproducible from the artifact. No fit, no comparison to any measured extraction curve, no verification of the closed-form solution against the ODEs.

## Assumptions and validity range
- Drip-filter geometry, hydrostatic (gravity-only) driving; nothing transfers to 9-bar pressure-driven espresso.
- Single lumped solute; bilinear kinetics with an undefined residual fraction λ; no intra-grain diffusion, no two-population fast/slow split, no saturation-region structure.
- Isothermal; constant θ, k; no infiltration/wetting phase (bed starts saturated); no bed deformation, fines migration, or swelling (authors list bed deformation and "chemical impact" as future work).
- Flow model is quasi-static: h(x) fixed, water height H fixed; vertical-flow-only in the averaged model; the mean-field advection closure hard-codes zero inlet concentration.
- Internal inconsistencies (Eqs. 1/3 conservation, Eq. 4 stray G, Eq. 7/6 k–κ collision, Eq. 8 h⁻¹ prefactor, undefined λ and C̃_v) mean the model as written cannot be implemented without editorial decisions the source does not license.
- Silent on: permeability provenance, exit/filter-paper resistance, PSD, temperature, multi-species chemistry, unsaturated flow.

## Overlaps and conflicts
- **moroney2016 (extraction, calibration) — supersedes this entirely.** Same problem (drip-filter brewing, closed-form extraction surrogate), same era, from the group whose Moroney 2015 model is the rigorous double-porosity treatment; moroney2016's asymptotic composite solutions do everything Eq. 5 here attempts, with defined symbols, dimensional hygiene, and a fitted dataset.
- **moroney2019 (flow/extraction/observables) — supersedes the geometry piece.** The conical/pour-over spatial-uniformity question (more extraction near top, geometry-driven gradients) is treated there with a verified CFD and a 1-D cone model, against real Δp measurements; the inclined-Darcy closed forms here are a sketch of the same idea with no validation.
- **cameron2020.extraction_bdf (extraction, runtime):** the bilinear (C_g − Gλ)(S − C_c) kinetics is a one-population, no-diffusion collapse of the registered two-population dissolution model; competes at strictly lower fidelity.
- **brewer2026.streamtube / lb_reference:** no contact — no heterogeneity, no microstructure.
- Backlog items touched: none. (The inclination-angle observation is drip-specific and outside every registry stage's espresso scope.)
