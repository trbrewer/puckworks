# Model card: Kim 2026 non-Newtonian anisotropic portafilter flow framework

**Paper:** Kim, R., "A Dynamical Analysis of Espresso Flow," unpublished manuscript / preprint, University of Texas at Austin, dated 18 Jan 2026. No DOI, no journal, no arXiv ID visible; author is an undergraduate (personal-site affiliation footnote). Cites grudeva2025 [their ref 1] and waszkiewicz2025 [their ref 2].
**Stage(s):** flow (secondary touches: bed_dynamics, observables) · **Kind:** runtime (as proposed)
**Status:** card-only

## Scope and mechanism
A purely theoretical framework paper: Darcy/Brinkman flow of a power-law fluid through the puck, with (i) a time-decaying, temperature-dependent viscosity ansatz, (ii) an anisotropic permeability tensor K, (iii) a heat equation with viscous dissipation coupled to the vertical flow, and (iv) a "normalized flux variance" S as a scalar channeling/uniformity metric on the outlet plane. Isotropic linearized case solved by separation of variables (Bessel series); anisotropic case deferred to unspecified numerics. No simulation is actually run, no parameter values are given, and no data of any kind appears. Contains several internal sign/consistency errors documented below.

## Governing equations
Numbering follows the paper.

1. Incompressible Navier–Stokes (Eq. 1): ρ(∂v/∂t + v·∇v) = −∇p + ∇·σ + f. Reduced by the author to ∂p/∂z ≈ ∇·σ under Re ≪ 1 with body force dropped.
2. Viscosity ansatz (Eq. 2): μ(t, γ̇, T) = γ̇^{n−1} (μ₀ e^{−Kt} + μ_M) e^{−b(T−T₀)}. Power-law shear index n; μ₀, μ_M initial/long-time consistency coefficients; K a time-decay rate (symbol collides with the permeability tensor K of Eq. 6, and with K₀ of Eq. 5); b thermal thinning coefficient; T₀ reference temperature. The time decay is asserted to represent crema/composition evolution — no mechanism, no data, no citation for the exponential form.
3. Darcy flow rate (Eqs. 3–4): Q = (kA/μ) Δp/Δh = (πR²k/μ) ∂p/∂z; v_z = (k/μ) ∂p/∂z, assuming v ≈ v_z ẑ. Note the POSITIVE sign convention (flow up the pressure gradient as written); the author acknowledges this convention in footnotes [8]–[9].
4. Shear-rate closure (unnumbered): γ̇ = α v_z / h, α a coefficient (not provided), h puck thickness.
5. Power-law Darcy velocity (Eq. 5): solving the implicit relation gives v_z = K₀ [∂_z p / (μ₀ e^{−Kt} + μ_M)]^{1/n} e^{bT/n}, K₀ = (h/α)^{(n−1)/n} (k/C₀)^{1/n}, C₀ = e^{bT₀}. Implies Q ∝ (Δp/Δh)^{1/n} — the standard power-law porous-media scaling.
6. Brinkman equation (unnumbered): the author derives ∇²v + (1/k)v = 0. This is WRONG in two ways relative to the standard Brinkman equation μ_e∇²v − (μ/k)v = ∇p: the drag term carries the wrong sign (+v/k acts as a gain, not a resistance), and the pressure gradient has vanished from the vector form. The Bessel-series solution below inherits both defects.
7. Anisotropic Darcy (Eqs. 6–7): v = (1/μ) K·∇p, K the (symmetric) permeability tensor; component form v_z = (1/μ)(K_zr ∂_r p + K_zθ (1/r)∂_θ p + K_zz ∂_z p). Total outflow Q(t) = ∫_{∂BΩ_P} v_z(r, θ, 0) dS (Eq. 8).
8. Thermal coupling (Eqs. 9–10): ρc_p(∂_t T + v_z ∂_z T) = κ∇²T + Φ, with dissipation Φ = 2μ D_ij D_ij written out in cylindrical components for v ≈ v_z ẑ. An entropy-consistency argument claims Φ = −v·∇p ≥ 0; under the paper's own positive sign convention v·∇p = (1/μ)(∇p)ᵀK(∇p) ≥ 0 for SPD K, which makes Φ ≤ 0 — the claimed sign check fails as written (footnote [9] waves this to convention).
9. Isotropic separable solution (Eq. 11): v_z = Σ_{λ,n} α_{λ,n} J_λ(m_{λ,n} r) Θ_λ(θ) Z_{λ,n}(z) · e^{bT}, with Θ trigonometric, Z exponential in z, Y_λ excluded by regularity at r = 0. Because of the Brinkman sign error, the correct linearized problem would produce modified Bessel functions I_λ/K_λ, not J_λ; the eigenstructure presented is not the solution of the physical drag problem.
10. Channeling metric (Eq. 12): S = √(∫(Q(r,θ,0) − ⟨Q⟩)² dS) / Q_t, discretized as S = σ_Q / Σ_n Q_n with σ_Q the standard deviation over N outlet partitions. As defined, S = σ_Q/(N⟨Q⟩), so it scales as 1/N with mesh refinement at fixed relative heterogeneity — it is not mesh-independent. (The mesh-independent version is the coefficient of variation σ_Q/⟨Q⟩.) The claimed delta-function limit S = 1 does not follow from the definition (σ of a squared delta diverges).

Boundary/initial conditions (§VI): no-slip at r = R, θ-periodicity, prescribed inlet velocity v_i and pressure p_i at z = d, v_z = 0 and T = T_i at t = 0. Outlet condition: threshold outflow v·n = g(r) only where p ≥ P_c (Eq. in §II).

Nothing has been simplified away in this transcription; the errors above are the paper's, flagged rather than repaired.

## Assumptions and validity range
- Re ≪ 1 laminar, vertical plug-direction flow v ≈ v_z ẑ — yet the anisotropic Darcy law (Eq. 7) generates radial/azimuthal velocities that the thermal and Brinkman treatments then ignore; the framework is internally inconsistent about its own anisotropy.
- Spatially uniform viscosity assumed to move μ through ∇· — inconsistent with the shear-rate-dependent μ(γ̇(r)) that motivates the power law.
- Saturated single-phase flow only; silent on wetting/infiltration, extraction (no solute transport at all), swelling/consolidation, fines migration, crema gas phase.
- Viscosity time-decay form and every coefficient are uncalibrated ansatz; no espresso rheometry is cited.
- Brinkman sign error and non-mesh-independent S metric (see equations 6 and 10 above) — the two concrete deliverables of the paper are the two things that are wrong.
- The θ-periodicity and outlet-threshold conditions are standard; the diffeomorphism machinery of §II does no work in the rest of the paper.
